ABOUT ME

Email: mukund(dot)bhole(at)tuwien(dot)ac(dot)at

In September 2021, I embarked on my journey toward the position of pre-doctoral researcher at #SafeSecLab in the Automation Systems Research Group at TU Wien, under the guidance of Prof. Wolfgang Kastner and Prof. Thilo Sauter. Currently, I am working on the project "Model-Based Safety and Security Evaluation of Operational Technology Components," which focuses on developing methods for testing the security and safety features of OT components in industrial control systems (ICS) based on engineering artifacts, particularly their specifications. The results of these tests will be assessed both qualitatively and quantitatively, with a focus on their potential impact. We are also investigating methods to automatically identify and evaluate countermeasures.

My journey to this position at #SafeSecLab began with a strong motivation to raise awareness about security and privacy. I completed my bachelor's degree from Shivaji University, Kolhapur (India), in 2019. The desire to pursue a career in security led me to earn a Master's degree in Information Security from the COEP Technological University, Pune , in 2021.

During my Master's program, I presented my thesis and project on "Developing a WiHART Communication Protocol Fuzzing Tool and Demonstrating Tool Usage for Penetration Testing." Additionally, I worked as a Graduate Research Analyst at Emerson Innovation Centre, Pune (India), where I gained knowledge and experience in Industrial Control Systems Security and Testing. My primary research interests include information security, penetration testing, industrial automation and control systems, and intrusion and vulnerability detection.
